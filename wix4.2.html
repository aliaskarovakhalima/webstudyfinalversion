Adding and Setting Up a Gallery

Add galleries to showcase images and encourage visitors to interact with your pages. Galleries have plenty of uses on your site â€“ you can display products or services, for example, or use the gallery like a portfolio. 
There are a variety of galleries to choose from, and you can adjust the settings to fit your desired look.

Step 1 | Adding a gallery

To get started, choose a gallery that fits your style from the Add panel. Depending on your needs, you can show your images in a grid, a slider, or a more unique layout like Honeycomb or Freestyle.
"component heading-component" data-component-type="heading">

To add a gallery:

  1. Click Add on the left side of the Editor.
  2. Click Gallery.
  3. Click a gallery type:
    • Grid Galleries: Choose one of these sleek, square-shaped galleries to let visitors see and browse all of your images at once.
    • Slider Galleries: These galleries let visitors browse through slides with your images, similar to a presentation.
    • More Galleries: Add a fun gallery to showcase your images in a unique way, with options like 3D Carousel, or Freestyle.
  4. Click and drag the gallery onto the page.

Step 2 | Adding images to your gallery

Once you've added your chosen gallery to your site, set it up by adding images. You can add free stock images with Wix and Unsplash, purchase stock images from Shutterstock, or upload your own.
Note:
The number of images you add affects your site loading time. We recommend splitting large galleries into separate smaller ones. 

To add images to your gallery:

  1. Click the gallery in your Editor.
  2. Click Change Images.
  3. Click + Add Images on top of the panel.
  4. Select a stock image, or images from your site files.
  5. Click Add to Gallery.
  6. Click Done.
Want to remove an image from your gallery?
Hover over the image in the Organize Your Gallery Images panel, and click the Remove Image icon . 

Step 3 | Adjusting your gallery settings

After adding images, use the settings to make the gallery and images behave the way you want. Depending on the gallery type, you can set what the images do when visitors click them, whether to display titles, and much more.  
Note:
Options displayed in the settings depend on the gallery type you choose.

To change gallery settings:

  1. Click the gallery in your Editor.
  2. Click the Settings icon .
  3. Adjust the gallery settings to your liking.

Cropping and Editing Gallery Images

The Wix Photo Studio allows you to crop and edit images on your site without leaving the Editor. Use it to enhance images, change their orientation and add filter effects!
Note:
You can only edit gallery images that you have uploaded yourself.

To crop or edit a gallery image:

  1. Click the gallery.
  2. Click Media Manager or Change Images (depending on the gallery you are using).
  3. Click Add Media.
  4. Click Image.
  5. Click the relevant image in the Media Manager.
  6. Click Crop & Edit.
  7. Edit your image using the tools.
     Note: Click here to learn about the editing tools.
  8. When you are finished, click Save.

About Buttons

Buttons are elements your visitors use to interact with your site. You can link them to a variety of locations such as your site pages, external sites, or even documents. Depending on the button type you choose, there are also a range of customization options available.

Start creating your buttons to make clear call to actions, and encourage your site visitors to explore what you or your brand has to offer.

Find a button for your needs

In the Editor, there are a variety of button types available for all of your site and business needs, so you can create a smooth interactive experience for your visitors. The buttons have a range of different uses and customization options to ensure that you can build a functional, sophisticated site without compromising on design. 
You can add the following buttons in the Editor:
Text & icon buttons
Text & icon buttons can contain both text and a vector image icon, and you can choose whether to display the text, icon, or both. These buttons are useful for directing visitors to other pages, information, or sites. A popular use for text & icon buttons is to lead visitors to your services or store pages. 

There are a range of styles and colors to add, so you don't need to choose between function and design. Learn how to add and set up your text & icon button

Icon buttons
Icon buttons are a fun, minimalist way to encourage visitors to interact with your site. There are hundreds of shapes and icons available in a variety of categories, so you have everything you need to create a clear call to action. 

Icon buttons are often used to direct visitors to your contact details and information.  
Learn how to add and set up an icon button on your site

Image buttons
Image buttons are an eye-catching way to direct your visitors to the information they need. They display unique effects when your visitors hover over them, which makes interacting with your site a fun, intriguing experience. 

Like text & icon buttons, they're useful for directing visitors to site pages and links. 
Learn how to add and set up an image button

Document buttons
Add and set up a document button to allow visitors to access and obtain files from your site. Document buttons make it easy for your visitors to download important information directly to their devices, which they can then send back to your or your business if needed. For example, you can link the button to an application form or contract. 

Learn how to add and set up a document button on your site

iTunes buttons
Use iTunes buttons to share your favorite music with your visitors. You set up the button by adding a iTunes link, so visitors are taken directly to the relevant playlist. These buttons are great for showcasing your own music, or even just the songs you love. 

Learn how to add and set up an iTunes button

Payment buttons
Payment buttons provide a smooth express checkout experience for your visitors. Connect the button to your site's payment methods to begin selling single items across your site, so your visitors can buy what they need in just a few clicks. 

Learn how to add and set up your pay button

PayPal buttons
PayPal buttons are a quick and useful way to accept payments on your site. You can add a Buy Now button to sell an item to visitors, or a Donate button to collect donations to you or your brand's organization. 

Since you connect the button directly to your PayPal account, you need to set up the button in the Editor to begin receiving payments. 
Learn more about PayPal buttons

Link your buttons to wherever you want

Add a link to your button to direct your site visitors to the relevant location. You can link to a variety different places and options, depending on your button type and site needs. The types of links you can add to a button are:
  • Page: Direct your visitors to a different page on your site.
  • Web address: Link your button to an external URL to bring visitors to another site.
  • Anchor: Link the button to an anchor on your site. When visitors click it, they are brought to the relevant page and anchor.
  • Top / bottom of page: When a visitor clicks this, they are brought to the top or bottom of the page they are currently on. 
  • Document: Link the button to a file you've uploaded to your site.
  • Email: Connect the button to your email address, so visitors can contact you directly from their browser or device.
  • Phone number: Link a button to your phone number to allow mobile or tablet visitors to call you instantly. Desktop visitors are taken to a calling app, such as FaceTime.
  • Lightbox: Link the button to your chosen lightbox on your site. 

Design your buttons to match your site

Customize your button in the Editor by clicking the Design icon to change the button's appearance, and the Layout icon to change its alignment or structure.
Depending on the button type you choose, you may be able to change the color and opacity of the button, as well as setting the alignment and spacing. You can edit as many or as little aspects as you like, so you have comprehensive control over designing a button that fits your brand and theme. 

Use buttons for advanced capabilities

With features such as Velo Dev Mode and the Content Manager, you can use buttons for advanced functions across your site, and create an upgraded, custom experience for your visitors.
By connecting buttons to collections in the Content Manager, you can define what happens when visitors click the button. One popular use of this is to set up buttons that collect input and information from visitors, such as files or text input. 

Enabling Velo Dev Mode on your site allows you to determine a button's behavior with code. For example, you can hide your button from the page until the visitor completes another action on your site, or set custom events.
