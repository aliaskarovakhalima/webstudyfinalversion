Definition and Usage

The <footer> tag defines a footer for a document or section.

A <footer> element typically contains:

  • authorship information
  • copyright information
  • contact information
  • sitemap
  • back to top links
  • related documents

You can have several <footer> elements in one document.

We need to add a footer to our portfolio with the website.
List of mandatory details:
1. Contact
2. Social networks
3. Address
4. List of important information
The footer or basement of the site (English footer) is a block at the very bottom of the page with information that may be useful to the visitor.
What to place in the footer of the site
In fact, many elements can be placed in the basement. However, they are usually limited to a few internal links and buttons of social networks. It is important to find a balance between functionality and simplicity. Next, we say what can be used in the basement and give qualitative examples.
3 required elements
There should be 3 things in each basement:
1. Privacy policy. A link to a document or page explaining how you will use and protect the personal data of visitors.
2. Terms of use. A link to the page where the general rules and recommendations governing the use of the site are described.
3. Copyright ©. This information makes it clear that copying information from your site will be considered plagiarism.
